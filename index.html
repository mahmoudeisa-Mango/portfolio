Financial Management · Ratio & DuPont Analysis · FY2023–2024

Reading Talaat Moustafa Group's balance sheet like a lender would

Pulled two fiscal years of financial statements from TMG — one of Egypt's largest listed real-estate developers — and worked through liquidity, solvency, profitability, market-value, and growth ratios to answer one question: is the balance sheet actually strong enough to support the growth it's chasing?

Total debt ratio 0.81 → 0.63 ROE 8.6% → 11.0% Net working capital '24 LE 62.8B Internal growth rate '24 3.74%

"TMG's 2024 sustainable growth rate of 10.85% runs well ahead of its 3.74% internal growth rate — the expansion is being carried by leverage and customer prepayments, not retained profit alone."

FILE NO. 02
Fieldwork

Research Methods · MGT222E · Quantitative Survey Study

What actually drives student satisfaction at AASTMT

Co-designed and ran a correlational study across AASTMT's branches to isolate what really moves the needle on student satisfaction: expectations, academic services, campus infrastructure, and word-of-mouth recommendations. Built the questionnaire, collected responses, then tested reliability and correlation before writing up the findings.

Design Cross-sectional, correlational Sample 76 respondents, multi-branch Scale 5-point Likert Variables tested 4

"Campus infrastructure came back with the strongest reliability of any variable in the study (α = 0.896) — the most consistently-felt driver of satisfaction across a genuinely mixed sample of local and international students."

Download full research paper (PDF) Literature review, methodology, SPSS-style output, and recommendations
